The Degree Distribution dialog

This dialog lets the user inspect the degree distribution of either the current network, or the mean degree distribution of the networks generated during a batch simulation. Since the networks of Laputa are directed, there are three distributions: that of ingoing edges, that of outgoing edges, and the combined sum of these.

Laputa supports fitting these distributions to curves, in order to find statistics of the networks it generates. Since such statistics are often only applied to the tail end of a distribution, a lower cutoff is specifiable through a slider. The Fit button selects the type of curve that best fits at the current cutoff. The specific curves are also selectable through a popup menu. At the moment, Laputa allows fitting the distribution to any of the following probability distribution types:

Normal: A normal distribution, with adjustable mean and standard deviation. Useful for modeling the result of independent events.
Poisson: A Poisson distribution, with adjustable parameter. Useful for modeling the result of independent events.
Yule-Simon: A Yule-Simon distribution, with adjustable shape parameter. The tail of a Yule-Simon distribution resembles a power law.
Zipf-Mandelbrot: A Zipf-Mandelbrot distribution, with three adjustable parameters. This is a typical form of power law, often used to model "rich-get-richer" type of processes.
Generalised Extreme Value: A Generalised extreme value distribution, with three adjustable parameters. This is a versatile form of distribution which includes the Frechet, Weibull and Gumbel families of distributions.
Generalised Beta Prime: A Generalised Beta Prime distribution, with four adjustable parameters. This is a versatile form of distribution which includes several others.
