Epistemic Value

Laputa automatically calculates the epistemic value of societies, both when simulated one-by-one, and when simulated in batch. How it is calculated depends on the epistemic value rules, which are accessible from the batch simulation dialog.

The Epistemic Value Rules dialog allows setting two aspects of the calculation: how a society's e-value is averaged over inquirers, and what utilities are assigned to which degrees of belief. For averaging over inquirers, the following options are available:

Individually: First the e-value of each individual inquirer's degree of belief is calculated, and then this e-value is averaged over the inquirers included in the statistics. The individual e-values are interpolated linearly from the values specified on the right. Setting Believe p to a, Believe p V ~p to b, and Believe ~p to c results in the E-value of the degree of belief x being

EV(x) = 2(x - 0.5)a + 2(1 - x)b

for x > 0.5,

EV(x) = 2xb + 2(0.5 - x)c

for x < 0.5, and

EV(x) = b

for x = 0.5.

To Average: First the average degree of belief in p

among the inquirers included in the statistics is calculated, and then the e-value of this degree of belief is calculated. The E-value is interpolated from the parameters to the right as in the individual calculation.

To Majority: The E-value of a society at a moment is set to 1 if a majority believe p, 0 if a majority believe not-p, and 0.5 otherwise. How many percent of the inquirers are required in order for it to count as a majority is determined by the field "req. %", and how high a credence in p an inquirer must have to count as believing p is determined by the fields "cert. %".
