Epistemic Value

Laputa automatically calculates the epistemic value of societies, both when simulated one-by-one, and when simulated in batch. How it is calculated depends on the epistemic value rules, which are accessible from the simulation and batch simulation dialogs.

The Epistemic Value Rules dialog allows setting two aspects of the calculation: how a society's e-value is averaged over inquirers, and how it is averaged over time. For inquirers, the following options are available:

Average: The e-value of a society at a moment is the average of the credences of the inquirers included in the statistics.

Majority: The e-value of a society at a moment is set to 1 if a majority believe p, 0 if a majority believe not-p, and 0.5 otherwise. How many percent of the inquirers are required in order for it to count as a majority is determined by the field "req. %", and how high a credence in p an inquirer must have to count as believing p is determined by the fiels "cert. %".

There are three methods available for averaging epistemic value over a simulation run's time:

Final The e-value is taken to be that of the final moment of the society.

Average The e-value is taken to be the average of that of the society at any time.

Attentuated This works like "average", but allows specification of an attentuation factor, which can be used to make earlier or later time steps count more than the others. It produces a weighted average, where each time step's weight is the attentuation factor specified in the field "att. %", raised to the step number. Setting it to 90%, for example, makes step 2 count as 90% as important as step 1, step 3 as 90% as important as step 2, etc.
